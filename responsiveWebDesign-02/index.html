SCANDAL

Spontaneus action lead to the longest running female rock band with the same musicians

SCANDAL is a Japanese pop-rock band known for their energetic performances and catchy, rock-inspired songs. Formed by four female high school friends, the band has achieved lasting success in Japan and internationally, especially among anime fans. Here’s a brief timeline of their awesome career:

  • 2006 - SCANDAL was formed with all the member still in highschool consist of Haruna Ono, Tomohi Ogawa, Rina Suzuki, and Mami Sasazaki
  • 2008 - Releases three indie singles—“Space Ranger,” “Koi Moyou,” and “Kagerou”—which gain popularity in Japan. The band also embarks on a "Japan Nite" U.S. tour, marking their international debut.
  • 2008 - SCANDAL signs with Epic Records Japan (Sony Music Japan) and releases their major debut single “DOLL”, which charts on Oricon and earns them attention nationwide.
  • 2009 - Releases “Shoujo S”, used as the opening theme for the anime Bleach, which catapults them to fame among anime fans. They release their first full album, “BEST★SCANDAL”, which reaches No. 5 on the Oricon charts.
  • 2009 - Nominated for the "Best New Artist Award" at the 51st Japan Record Awards. They lost to BIG BANG but still won the "New Artist Award" (all "Best New Artist Award" nominees receive the "New Artist Award").
  • 2010 - Releases “Shunkan Sentimental”, the fourth ending theme for Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood, which further boosts their popularity worldwide.
  • 2011 - Releases their second album, “Temptation Box,” and wins the "Best New Artist" award at the Japan Record Awards. They also release “Love Survive” and a series of successful singles, building a solid fan base.
  • 2012 - Releases the album “Queens are Trumps – Kirifuda wa Queen”, marking a shift toward a more mature rock sound. They also embark on their first Asian tour, solidifying their international fanbase.
  • 2013 - Their fifth album, “Standard,” is released, including songs written by all band members, showcasing their evolving sound.
  • 2014 - SCANDAL becomes the first Japanese girl band to perform at Osaka-Jo Hall, a significant venue. They release “Hello World,” an album that features tracks with more experimentation and self-written lyrics.
  • 2015 - Embarks on their first world tour, performing across North America, Europe, and Asia, increasing their global following.
  • 2016 - Releases their seventh album, “YELLOW”, a self-produced album that marks their growth as independent artists.
  • 2017 - SCANDAL celebrates their 11th anniversary with various concerts and releases “SCANDAL,” a compilation album of their greatest hits.
  • 2018 - Established their own independent label, her.
  • 2019 - Release their ninth album "Kiss from the darkness".
  • 2020-2021 - Continues releasing singles, such as “Living in the City” and “Ivory,” connecting with fans virtually due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • 2022 - SCANDAL WORLD TOUR 2022 "MIRROR" in Japan, America, and Europe.
  • 2023 - Held SCANDAL 17th ANNIVERSARY 『Sekai Ichi』 on their 17th anniversary, and also set the Guinness World Record for "Longest running rock band with the same musicians (female)."
